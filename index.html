An advocacy of the non-binary

"We are in charge of our perception of reality and that we hold the means to create, reinforce, or alter that perceived reality."

-Lori Girshwick, author of "Transgender Voices"

Is gender truly as binary as we are made to believe? That we are either a man or a woman?


Can you be a man and a woman at the same time? Can you be none at all?

Gender is a socially constructed understanding that forms the basis for shared assumptions about reality.


Developed and institutionalised, the gender binary has become widely accepted as "natural" and made into tradition by the society.

But where do you fit in all this?

To start with, sex and gender are two separate concepts. Sex refers to your biological anatomy, and gender is an inner sense of identity that is subjective and personal to each individual.

    We’ve all been told that there are only two options: man or woman, but you must've gotten a fair idea by now that those aren’t the only options.


    There’s an amalgam of multiple identities and expressions that give rise to diversity and several possibilities on the wide spectrums of sex and gender.

    If the first digit stood for the presence of male biological traits in the body, and the second digit stood for the presence of female biological traits in the body (with the value 1 signifying yes or true, and the value 0 signifying no or false) we can map four possibilities on a chart like this:

    0 1
    0 00 01
    1 10 11
  • 00 : ambiguous
  • 01 : female
  • 10 : male
  • 11 : intersex

    • For gender identity, similarly, the first two digits stand for the presence of masculinity & femininity, but let us also assume there's a third digit that stands for fluidity (the feeling of fluctuation or fluidity between more than one genders). We can map all the eight possibilites across a chart that looks like this:

    0 1
    00 000 001
    01 010 011
    10 100 101
    11 110 111
  • 000 : neutrois
  • 001 : agender
  • 010 : woman
  • 011 : demigirl
  • 100 : man
  • 101 : demiboy
  • 110 : multigender
  • 111 : genderfluid

    • Taking all these possibilities (biological sex across x, and gender identity across y), we end up with a chart much bigger—one that contains wider variation and accounts for an extensive range beyond the cisnormative binary.

    0 1

  • 00 : non-conforming
  • 01 : feminine
  • 10 : masculine
  • 11 : androgynous

    • Aggregating all of the above, we get a 3-dimensional matrix with a total of 128 different ways of identitifying and expressing our genders and bodies.
