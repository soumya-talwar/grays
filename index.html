An advocacy of the non-binary

"We are in charge of our perception of reality and that we hold the means to create, reinforce, or alter that perceived reality."

-Lori Girshwick, author of "Transgender Voices"

Is gender truly as binary as we are made to believe? That we are either a man or a woman?


Can you be a man and a woman at the same time? Can you be none at all?

Gender is a socially constructed understanding that forms the basis for shared assumptions about reality.


Developed and institutionalised, the gender binary has become widely accepted as "natural" and made into tradition by the society.

But where do you fit in all this?

To start with, sex and gender are two separate concepts. Sex refers to your biological anatomy, and gender is an inner sense of identity that is subjective and personal to each individual.


To help you understand a little more about gender, and about yourself, let me ask you a few blocks.

    Agender

    Agender

    People who identify as agender may describe themselves as genderless, genderfree, or as someone who does not know or care about gender as an internal identity or an external label.

    Androgyny

    Androgyny

    A gender presentation that lies ambiguously between or combines the traditional masculine and feminine.

    Assigned Gender

    Assigned Gender

    Assigned gender is the gender one is assigned at birth, typically on the basis of their birth genitalia from either one of the two primary binary genders most commonly used in western society: man and woman.

    Assigned Sex

    Assigned Sex

    The sex you were interpreted as at birth, which usually corresponds to the gender identity you were raised as and/or assumed to have in childhood. As a phrase, this is a way to refer to the sex that was put on your birth certificate. In cultures with a gender binary, assigned sex is either male or female. Assigned sex is often referred to using the acronyms AFAB (Assigned Female At Birth) or AMAB (Assigned Male At Birth).

    Bigender

    Bigender

    A person who experiences exactly two gender identities, either simultaneously or varying between the two. The person might experience the feeling of being a “man” and a “woman”, but could also include other non binary identities.

    Biological Sex

    Biological Sex

    A medical term used to refer to the chromosomal, hormonal and anatomical characteristics that are used to classify an individual as female or male or intersex. Often referred to as simply “sex,” “physical sex,” “anatomical sex,” or specifically as “sex assigned at birth.”

    Cisgender

    Cisgender

    A person whose gender identity and biological sex assigned at birth align (for example: somebody who was assigned male at birth and identifies as a man).

    Cisnormativity

    Cisnormativity

    The assumption, in individuals or in institutions, that everyone is cisgender, and that cisgender identities are superior to trans identities or people. Cisnormativity leads to invisibility of non-cisgender identities.

    Demiboy

    Demiboy

    A demiboy (also called a demiman or demimale person) is a gender identity describing someone who partially identifies as a man.

    Demigender

    Demigender

    People who have a partial connection to a certain gender. This includes the partly female identity demigirl, and the partly male identity demiboy.

    Demigirl

    Demigirl

    Also called a demiwoman or a demifemale person, Demigirl is a gender identity describing someone who partially identifies as a woman.

    Female

    Female

    A female is often associated with female sex characteristics such as a vagina, a uterus, wide hips, breasts and a high-pitched voice.

    Feminine Expression

    Feminine Expression

    Set of behaviours, presentations and roles which are culturally associated with being a woman and/or possessing female sex characteristics. Attributes which are often associated with femininity include grace, delicacy, gentleness, passivity, nurturing instincts, a desire to care for others, and a gender expression which includes dresses or skirts, long hair and/or make-up.

    Gender Binary

    Gender Binary

    The belief, prejudice or social force that claims only two genders exist—male and female—and that all non-binary and genderqueer gender identities are invalid.

    Gender Conformation Surgery

    Gender Binary

    Surgery that alters a person’s appearance to better reflect their preferred gender presentation.

    Gender Dysphoria

    Gender Dysphoria

    A feeling of discomfort, stress, confusion or negativity that is caused by a mismatch between one’s assigned sex and one’s actual gender, often accompanied by a strong desire to change one's sex to better match their identity or to be called the correct gendered language.

    Gender Expression

    Gender Expression

    External manifestation of gender that is expressed through a person’s name, pronouns, clothing, behavior, and/or body characteristics.

    Gender Identity

    Gender Identity

    A person’s internal, deeply held sense of their gender. Gender identity is an internal experience; it cannot be assigned, diagnosed, measured or disproved by anyone else. A person's gender identity is their own.

    Gender Presentation

    Gender Presentation

    The way that a person’s gender superficially appears to onlookers, which may be affected by anatomy, clothing, makeup, hairstyle, speech patterns and body language.

    Gender Roles

    Gender Roles

    Set of societal norms dictating the types of behaviors which are generally considered acceptable, appropriate, or desirable for people based on their assigned sex. Gender roles are usually centered on conceptions of femininity and masculinity.

    Genderfluid

    Genderfluid

    A person who experiences different genders at different times, with different people and/or situations. They may at any time identify as a man, woman, agender, or any other non-binary identity, or even a combination of identities.

    Genderqueer

    Genderqueer

    An umbrella term with a similar meaning to non-binary. It can be used to describe any gender identities other than man and woman, thus outside of the gender binary.

    Intersex

    Intersex

    An intersex individual possesses a combination of chromosomes, gonads, hormones, internal sex organs, and genitals that that do not fit the traditional definition of male or female.

    LGBTQIA+

    LGBTQIA+

    Lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and others. An acronym for the cultural and political community of people who are not heterosexual, hetero-romantic and cisgender. Sometimes expanded up to LGBT*QQIAUP+, in which T* = all transgender, non-binary and genderqueer people; Q = queer/questioning; I = intersex; A = asexual; U = undecided; and P = pansexual.

    Male

    Male

    A male is often associated with male sex characteristics such as a penis, testes, narrow hips, a prominent Adam's apple and a low-pitched voice.

    Man

    Man

    A gender identity that is part of the gender binary. Men may be cisgender (assigned male at birth and identifying as man) or transgender (assigned female at birth and identifying as man).

    Masculine Expression

    Masculine Expression

    Set of behaviours, presentations and expression which are culturally associated with being a man and/or possessing male sex characteristics. Attributes which are often associated with masculinity include physical strength, power, assertiveness, aggression, social independence and a gender expression which includes trousers, short hair and/or visible musculature.

    Multigender

    Multigender

    A term for anyone who experiences more than one gender identity. It can be used as a gender identity in its own right, or can be an umbrella term for other identities which fit this description. Multigender identities include bigender (two genders), trigender (three genders), quadgender (four genders), quintgender (five genders), polygender (many genders), pangender (all genders) and genderfluid (variable gender). Multigender people may experience two or more gender identities at the same time, or their gender identity may change over time (genderfluid). The gender identities they experience may be male, female, non-binary, or genderless.

    Neutrois

    Neutrois

    A non-binary gender identity which is often associated with a feeling of a 'neutral' or a 'null' gender that is neither masculine, feminine, nor anything in between, but still feeling strongly gendered.

    Non-Binary

    Non-Binary

    Non-Binary describes any gender identity which does not fit the male and female binary. Those with non-binary genders can feel that they: have an androgynous (both masculine and feminine) gender identity, have an identity between male and female, have a neutral or unrecognized gender identity, such as agender or neutrois, have multiple gender identities, such as bigender or pangender, have a gender identity which varies over time, known as genderfluid, have a weak or partial connection to a gender identity, known as demigender, are intersex and identify as intersex, known as amalgagender.

    Pangender

    Pangender

    The Greek prefix “pan” refers to “everything” or “all”. Pangender describes a person who experiences all genders. It is a non-binary gender identity which refers to a experiencing vast and diverse multiplicity of genders in the same individual that can extend infinitely, always within the person's own culture and life experience. Pangender is very expansive and unspecific, it is feeling an entire infinite gender spectrum that is possible for an individual to have.

    Pronouns

    Pronouns

    A form of gendered language which refers to a person through words other than their name. Although some pronouns are traditionally associated with certain genders, anyone of any gender identity can use any pronouns. Some examples include- She/her/hers, He/him/his, They/them/theirs, Ey/em/eirs, Xe/xem/xyrs, Ze/zir/zirs.

    Questioning

    Questioning

    An individual who or time when someone is unsure about or exploring their gender identity.

    Queer

    Queer

    An umbrella term for anyone whose internal experience of gender does not match the gender they were assigned at birth (normally based on genitalia).

    Transition

    Transition

    To change your presentation in order to reflect a gender different from the one assigned to you at birth either to be more congruent with the gender/sex you feel yourself to be and/or to be in harmony with your preferred gender expression. Transitioning may include, but does not require, any of the following: changing one’s pronouns, wearing different clothing than before, altering one’s legal gender, taking hormone therapy, and undergoing surgery.

    Transphobia

    Transphobia

    The fear of, discrimination against, or hatred of trans* people, the trans* community, or gender ambiguity.

    Woman

    Woman

    A gender identity which is part of the gender binary. Women may be cisgender (assigned female at birth and identifying as women) or transgender (assigned male at birth and identifying as women).
